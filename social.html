UCSC DUCKS

I secretly hid tiny resin ducks around UCSC's campus—nestled in buildings, libraries, forests, residential areas, and beyond—posting clues on social media to turn each find into a mini adventure. It wasn’t just about finding a duck; it was about creating a playable experience where people could actively participate in the hunt, explore hidden corners of the campus, and rediscover its charm. As students and staff followed the clues and unearthed these tiny treasures, I became an anonymous celebrity— the elusive "duck-hiding vigilante." Through this playful quest, I sparked a sense of adventure, turning UCSC into a living treasure hunt and reviving the walking culture that had slowly faded.

Aliens Rising

Inspired by the popular TikTok accounts that use AI art to craft storytelling videos and immersive "Would You Rather" scenarios, I decided to try creating my own. I am aware that marketing products, especially games, on social media can be a challenge, so I saw this as an opportunity to practice connecting with a niche — in this case supernatural themes, something I’m passionate about. By experimenting with AI-generated videos, I aimed to appeal to like-minded individuals and foster a community of shared interests that I can market products to (as much as I want for free). Ultimately, this is a stepping stone toward building a platform for advertising games or other projects to an audience that shares my enthusiasm.
