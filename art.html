Level Design

In the development of my game BALLISTIC, I dove into learning Blender for level design, and my skills have improved tremendously. Over time, I've become highly proficient at using Blender, experimenting with new tools and assets to create unique, engaging environments. My friends often call me a "Blender wizard" because of how quickly I master new shortcuts, techniques, and software features. I'm always eager to learn and push my boundaries with Blender, which allows me to work faster and more efficiently, bringing fresh ideas to life in my game designs.

Pixels

I've been passionate about pixel art for years, creating a wide variety of assets and artwork using Aseprite. My friends and I even have "pixel jam" sessions, where we draw together and share screens, exploring new shading techniques and gaining fresh perspectives from each other. One of my proudest innovations is the concept of a "Pixel Playground"—a space where we brainstorm and theorize about a game’s visual style, while simultaneously creating assets to bring that vision to life in Aseprite. We’ve even used the Pixel Playground as an informal form of version control, ensuring our creative process remains dynamic and collaborative.
