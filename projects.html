BALLISTIC

BALLISTIC is a my capstone project for UCSC. I knew from the day I applied to the college that I intended to strive to make one of the best games in the program. I spent the time leading up to the project practicing leading development teams, honing skills regarding pipelining and efficient work flows as well as a strong sense of design. Using these, I was able to successfully lead my team in making BALLISTIC, a 10 player multiplayer dodgeball action party game.

A sneak peek at one of my latest projects in development: an infinitely left-and-right-laned endless runner where you take on the role of a daring driver, weaving through traffic and evading cars. I'm excited to announce that a global leaderboard powered by Firebase is almost complete, alongside plans for diverse car types and additional exciting features. The game is on track for a mobile release by the end of February. If you're curious about my other projects and experiments, be sure to check out my blog for updates and behind-the-scenes insights.

Mindful Meadow

I've taken a huge interest in GAMIFIED LEARNING recently. My most active project currently is this gamified mental health and daily planner app called Mindful Meadow*. Users can complete daily goals and are rewarded with beans that they can use to buy things from eventually many different shops. They will be able to customize their homes and adopt pets that roam around! I did Heavy amounts of market research studying apps predominantly like Finch, Habitica, Quabble, Habit Rabbit, and more, deciphering what users like and don't like from many reviews to ensure our app is as great as it can be, and ultimately be a game changer for mental health resources. I intend to use Godot for the project since it is more light weight and should not require heavy graphics or physics.

Potion Quest

A friend and I started a game development team called Sailboat Studio. We actively use this team to practice streamlining synchronous and asynchronous communication and workflows to make experimental projects and generally grow as programmers. One of our recent projects for the Pirate Software Game Jam was Potion Quest. The game consists of fully original 3d models, strong puzzle design, unique shaders, procedural animation, and more! We completed this project in around 8 days.
