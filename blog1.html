Good Design

Some Recent Thoughts

Good design is fundamentally about creating intuitive and engaging experiences for users. One of my professors, Tyler Coleman, described this concept as the "juice" of a game—the small, thoughtful details that add polish and bring a project to life. These might include subtle animations, responsive hover effects, or smooth transitions that make an interface feel fluid and dynamic. While the term originates from game design, it applies equally well to any form of digital media. Juice reflects a creator’s attention to detail and effort to elevate a project beyond functionality, making it memorable and enjoyable to interact with.

Documentation, on the other hand, is a more complex and often overlooked challenge. Effective documentation takes time and practice to master, but the results are invaluable. High-quality content is essential for a compelling portfolio. Poor documentation—like capturing dimly lit gameplay footage of Skyrim with glitches and uninspired dialogue—fails to convey the strengths of a project. In contrast, a well-curated Pac-Man highlight reel with clear and exciting visuals leaves a lasting impression. I’ve struggled with this myself, having neglected to collect strong visuals and clips for past projects. Going forward, I plan to prioritize documenting my work consistently, capturing images and videos as I progress. This past year, I did focus on written documentation to track my learning, and it’s already proven to be a powerful resource for revisiting and applying past knowledge.

I’ve also come to believe that improvement is an ongoing process. Growing up, I spent countless hours painting and creating with friends, and I realized that no artwork is ever truly complete. There’s always an opportunity to add depth or refine details. This perspective drives my approach to digital media; a constant commitment to refinement can be seen and felt by anyone who interacts with the work. I’m dedicating time each week to improving my portfolio, aiming to strike a balance between compelling content and thoughtful design. Incorporating user feedback and studying others’ portfolios will further guide my progress. This iterative process ensures that every project I create reflects both the care and the creativity I bring to my work.
